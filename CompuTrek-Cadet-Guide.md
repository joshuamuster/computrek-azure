# CompuTrek Cadet Field Guide

Welcome aboard the **C.S.S. Curiosity**, Cadet. This guide is your orientation manual — a quick tour of every station you'll use during your training. Read it once, then keep it handy until the controls feel like second nature.

> **Tip:** You are a *Cadet*. Teachers and staff are *crew officers*. Your missions, grades, and typing records are all tied to your cadet record.

---

## Logging In

On the login screen, choose your role and enter your **PIN or password**. As you start typing, the **"Make It So"** button comes alive with a glow — that's your signal it's ready. Press it (or hit Enter) to board the ship.

---

## The Bridge (Home Screen)

The home screen is your command center. Across the top is the **HUD** — a row of live status panels:

- **Stellar Chronology** — The ship's clocks. Universal Time is the real clock, the Mission Clock counts down to the end of the period, and the Objective Timer counts down the current activity.
- **Typing Report** — A snapshot of your typing speed (WPM) and accuracy, with your numbers next to the class average.
- **Systems** — The ship diagram. Click it to jump to the **Ship's Systems** page.
- **Mission Reports** — How much work you've turned in and how much has been accepted, compared to the class.
- **Settings** — Opens the **Terminal Settings** panel (zoom, fullscreen, log out).
- **Opening** — Plays the title sequence. Just for fun. 🖖

Below the HUD you'll find your welcome briefing and a **Meet the Crew** card.

---

## Cadet Dashboard

This is the most important page for staying on top of your work. It's split into three columns:

- **Assigned** — Missions waiting for you to start or finish. *Empty means you're all clear!*
- **Awaiting Grade** — Missions you've submitted that your teacher hasn't graded yet. If a due date shows up **red**, that mission was turned in late.
- **Overdue** — Missions past their due date that still need to be submitted. Take care of these first.

At the top is your personal **status strip**: your current **grade**, your **typing tier**, and your **conduct** score. If anything slips past the submission window, it moves to **Lost in Space** — talk to your teacher about an extension.

Click any mission card to open it, read the instructions, and submit your work.

---

## Typing Lab

Your training ground for building typing speed and accuracy. Every session is saved to your record.

- **Lessons** — Structured drills that build from the home row up. Work through them in order.
- **Speed Test** — A timed test to measure your words-per-minute.
- **Custom Text** — Special practice passages your teacher posts (only appears when something's available).

---

## Tactical Simulator (Games)

A holodeck of Star Trek–themed games — chess, minesweeper, and more. These aren't just for fun: they sharpen logic, patience, and problem-solving. Click a game to read its briefing, then launch it.

---

## Ship's Systems

A live diagram of the C.S.S. Curiosity. Each section of the ship is color-coded to show how your **class** is doing overall — blue and green mean systems are running well, yellow and red mean things need attention. Click a section to read its status report.

---

## Courses

Your lesson content lives here, organized by **unit** and **lesson**. This is where you'll find the slides, instructions, and activities for what you're learning in class. Navigate into a unit, then pick a lesson to begin.

---

## Crew

The ship's personnel directory. Meet your instructors, support staff, and fellow cadets aboard the Curiosity. A good place to learn who does what.

---

## Documents

Important PDFs — like the course **syllabus** — that you can view, download, or print. Click any document to open it.

---

## Terminal Settings

Opened from the **Settings** panel on the HUD. From here you can:

- **Adjust display zoom** — Make text bigger or smaller (handy on a student laptop).
- **Fullscreen** — Expand the app to fill your screen.
- **Log Out** — End your session when you're done. Always log out on shared computers!

---

## Quick Tips for a Successful Mission

1. **Check your Dashboard first** every class — knock out anything in Overdue.
2. **Submit your work** through the mission card, not just by finishing it.
3. **Practice typing** a little each day — small gains add up fast.
4. **Log out** when you leave a shared computer.
5. **Stuck?** Hitting a dead end means you're learning. Ask a crewmate or your teacher.

*Live long and code well, Cadet.* 🖖
